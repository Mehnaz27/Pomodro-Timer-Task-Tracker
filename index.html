    How this works

    Focusroom pairs a 25-minute focus timer with a simple task list, built around the Pomodoro Technique — a time-management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s.

    The Pomodoro Technique, in short

    The idea is to work in short, fully-focused bursts instead of grinding for hours at a stretch. Each burst is called a "pomodoro," named after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer Cirillo originally used. You commit to one task, work without interruption until the timer ends, then take a short break before starting the next one. The forced breaks keep concentration high and make long tasks feel less overwhelming.

    Using the timer

    • Start begins the countdown for the current phase (focus or break).
    • Pause (same button, relabeled) temporarily stops the countdown without losing progress.
    • Reset stops the timer and returns the current phase to its full length.
    • When a focus session finishes, the screen flashes, a short tone plays, and Focusroom moves straight into a 5-minute break.
    • When a break finishes, Focusroom switches back to a fresh 25-minute focus session — press Start whenever you're ready to begin it.
    • Your completed-session count for today is shown as filled dots and resets automatically at midnight.

    Using the task list

    • Type a task into the field and press Add (or hit Enter).
    • Click the square beside a task to mark it complete — it stays visible with a line through it.
    • Click the × beside a task to remove it permanently.
    • Tasks and your session count are saved in this browser automatically, so refreshing or closing the page won't lose your progress.
