    readme.txt

    pomodoro.sh runs the Pomodoro Technique, a time-management method Francesco Cirillo developed in the late 1980s, named after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer he used to time his work.

    the technique

    Work happens in short, fully-focused bursts called "pomodoros," separated by short breaks. Committing to one task for a fixed 25 minutes — with no switching, no notifications — keeps concentration high, and the built-in breaks stop long work sessions from wearing you down.

    01.pick one task from tasks.txt to work on
    02.run ./start and focus for 25 minutes straight
    03.when the timer hits 00:00, a 5-minute break starts automatically
    04.step away, then run ./start again to begin your next focus block

    timer commands

    • ./start begins the countdown for the current mode (focus or break)
    • ./start becomes ./pause mid-run — press it again to pause without losing progress
    • ./reset stops the timer and restores the current mode to its full length
    • when a focus block ends: the screen flashes, a tone plays, and the terminal switches straight into a 5-minute break
    • when a break ends: the terminal resets to a fresh 25-minute focus block, paused and waiting on you to run ./start
    • sessions_today.log fills in one LED per completed focus session, and clears itself at midnight

    tasks.txt commands

    • type into the input and press add (or Enter) to append a task
    • click the [ ] beside a task to toggle it to [x] complete
    • click the × to delete a task permanently
    • tasks and today's session count are written to this browser's storage automatically — closing or refreshing the tab won't lose them
